About British Airways

The United Kingdom’s major air carrier is "British Airways". British Airways maintains its headquarters in London, England, besides its main hub at Heathrow Airport. British Airways is ranked as the second-largest airline, measured by its fleet and the number of passengers carried by the airline. It serves both domestic and international flights at a very Pocket- friendly price. Furthermore, the airline has a codeshare agreement with about 25 other carriers.

British Airways Carry – on Baggage Policy

British Airways has different types of baggage policies for carry-on as Well as Checked baggage.

Carry-on Baggage

One free small personal item and one free carry-on bag are provided to each passenger.

The weight limit for the free carry-on bag is 23 kg, or 56 cm* 45 cm * 25 cm (22*17.7*9.8) in size.

The weight limit for the complimentary small private item is 23 kg, or approximately 40 cm*15 cm*30 cm (15.7*5.9*11.8).

Checked Baggage

The passenger's flight into the airport will have a total impact on the checked baggage measurement.

Economy Class: up to a maximum size and one or two pieces of three checked bags.

On all flights, premium economy passengers are entitled to two complimentary checked bags with a combined weight limit of 23 kg.

On all flights, business class passengers are entitled to two complimentary checked bags with a combined weight limit of 32 kg.

On all flights, First Class passengers are entitled to three complimentary checked bags with a combined maximum weight of 32 kilogrammes.

Check- in

Passengers can do online check – in or can do Airport check – in.

Web –check – in

On the official British Airways page or on our website, travellers can check in online for their flight. Online check-in begins 24 hours prior to the scheduled departure time of the flight.

Airport Check – in

For those Passengers that can’t check in online, they can check in via airport check-in, which can be done within the reach of the airport with some relevant options.
